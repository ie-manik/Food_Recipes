Chicken Biryani

Prep: 30 min  |  Cook: 60 min  |  Serves: 4  |  Difficulty: Hard

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked 30 min)
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 4 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 inch ginger (grated)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cardamom pods
  • Saffron strands (soaked in warm milk)
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
← Back to All Recipes

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Marinate the chicken with yogurt, biryani masala, red chilli powder, turmeric, garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.

2

Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Fry the sliced onions on medium heat, stirring frequently, until they turn deep golden brown and crispy (about 15 minutes). Remove half the fried onions and set aside for layering.

3

In the same pot with remaining onions, add bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon, and cardamom. Sauté for 1 minute. Add the marinated chicken and cook on high heat for 5 minutes, then reduce to medium and cook until the chicken is 80% done and the oil separates (about 15 minutes).

4

While the chicken cooks, parboil the soaked basmati rice in a large pot of boiling salted water with a bay leaf until the rice is 70% cooked (the grain should still have a bite in the centre). Drain immediately and set aside.

5

Layer the parboiled rice over the chicken in the pot. Sprinkle the reserved fried onions, fresh mint leaves, coriander leaves, and the saffron-soaked milk evenly over the rice. Drizzle 1 tbsp of ghee on top.

6

Cover the pot tightly with a lid (you can seal the edge with dough for the authentic dum method). Cook on high heat for 5 minutes, then reduce to the lowest heat and cook for 25 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before gently mixing and serving with raita.
