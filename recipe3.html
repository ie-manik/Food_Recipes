Paneer Tikka

Prep: 15 min  |  Cook: 25 min  |  Serves: 3  |  Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 300g paneer (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup thick curd (yogurt)
  • 1 tbsp besan (gram flour)
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 capsicum (cubed)
  • 1 onion (cubed)
  • Chaat masala for serving
← Back to All Recipes

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

In a large mixing bowl, combine the thick curd, besan, red chilli powder, turmeric, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, lemon juice, oil, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Whisk everything together into a smooth marinade.

2

Add the cubed paneer, capsicum pieces, and onion cubes to the marinade. Gently mix so all pieces are well coated. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours for best flavour).

3

Thread the marinated paneer, capsicum, and onion alternately onto skewers. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 15 minutes beforehand so they don't burn.

4

Preheat your grill or oven to 220°C (430°F). Place the skewers on a greased baking tray or grill rack. Brush lightly with oil.

5

Grill or bake for 15-20 minutes, turning once halfway through, until the paneer has slight char marks on the edges and the vegetables are slightly softened and blistered.

6

Remove from the oven and immediately sprinkle with chaat masala. Serve hot with mint chutney, sliced onion rings, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
